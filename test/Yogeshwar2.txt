1. Dehradun and Mussoorie — Where Nature and Tranquillity Embrace Each Other

Dehradun and Mussoorie, located in the breathtaking state of Uttarakhand, are two enchanting destinations that offer a perfect blend of natural beauty and serenity. Dehradun, the capital city, is nestled in the Doon Valley and is renowned for its pleasant climate and colonial charm. On the other hand, Mussoorie, often referred to as the “Queen of Hills,” is a hill station known for its picturesque landscapes and mist-covered mountains.

Why visit:

Visiting Dehradun and Mussoorie is an absolute delight for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and those seeking a peaceful getaway. These destinations offer a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the lap of nature and rejuvenate their senses. From lush green valleys to cascading waterfalls, these places have something for everyone.

Ideal for:

Families, honeymooners, nature lovers, and adventure enthusiasts.

The destinations offer a wide range of activities and attractions that cater to diverse interests and age groups.

Best time to visit:

(April to June — Summer & July to September — Monsoon Season)

The best time to visit Dehradun and Mussoorie is during the summer months, from April to June, when the weather is pleasant and the natural beauty is at its peak. Additionally, the monsoon season, from July to September, enhances the charm of these places with lush greenery and gushing waterfalls.

Ideal duration:

To fully explore the beauty of Dehradun and Mussoorie, it is recommended to plan a trip for 4 to 5 days. This duration allows ample time to visit popular attractions, engage in outdoor activities, and soak in the serene ambiance of the region.

How to reach:

Both Dehradun and Mussoorie are well-connected to major cities in India. The most convenient way to reach these destinations is by air, with flights operating from major cities to Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun. Alternatively, one can also reach by road through well-maintained highways or by train to Dehradun Railway Station.

Things to do:

- Explore Robber’s Cave in Dehradun, a natural cave formation surrounded by hills and a flowing river.

- Take a cable car ride to Gun Hill in Mussoorie for panoramic views of the Himalayas.

- Visit Kempty Falls, a popular waterfall near Mussoorie, and enjoy a refreshing dip in its cool waters.

- Indulge in trekking and nature walks in the lush green trails of Dehradun and Mussoorie.

- Discover the architectural marvels of Mussoorie, such as Christ Church and Landour Clock Tower.

Places to stay:

Dehradun and Mussoorie offer a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular options include luxury hotels and resorts like JW Marriott Mussoorie Walnut Grove Resort & Spa, budget hotels like Hotel Ashrey, and dharamshalas for those seeking a spiritual experience.

Safety tips:

- Carry necessary medications and first aid kit, especially if you plan to engage in outdoor activities or trekking.

- Dress appropriately, considering the weather conditions and cultural sensitivities of the region.

- Stay hydrated and carry a water bottle with you, especially during outdoor excursions.

- Follow guidelines and instructions while visiting natural attractions to ensure your safety and the preservation of the environment.

- Be cautious of your belongings and avoid venturing into isolated areas alone, especially at night.

Nearby attractions:

- Dhanaulti: A serene hill station known for its panoramic views and adventure activities.

- Haridwar: A holy city famous for its religious significance and the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti.

- Rishikesh: The Yoga Capital of the World, offering spirituality, adventure sports, and serene river views.

- Rajaji National Park: A wildlife sanctuary where you can spot diverse flora and fauna, including elephants and tigers.

- Mussoorie Lake: A peaceful lake where you can enjoy boating and picnicking.

2. Nainital And Ranikhet — Where tranquillity Meets Serenity

Nainital, often referred to as the “Lake District of India” is a picturesque hill station known for its beautiful lakes and lush green surroundings. Ranikhet, located in the Almora district, is a charming hill station that offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

Nainital and Ranikhet, with their scenic beauty, serene atmosphere, and abundant attractions, are the perfect getaway destinations in Uttarakhand. Whether you seek quietness, adventure, or a blend of both, these hill stations offer a memorable experience. Plan your trip, immerse yourself in the breathtaking landscapes, indulge in local cuisine, and create lasting memories in the lap of the Himalayas.

Why Visit

Nainital and Ranikhet are popular getaways due to their natural beauty, pleasant climate, and numerous tourist attractions. Nainital is famous for its shimmering lakes, including the Naini Lake, which is surrounded by hills and offers boating opportunities. Ranikhet is known for its panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks and its serene atmosphere.

Ideal For Nature lovers, honeymooners, and families seeking a peaceful retreat amidst the hills. The serene environment and pleasant weather make these places perfect for a relaxing vacation.

Ideal Duration — A minimum of 3–4 days.

Best Time to Visit

(March to June — Summer & October to February — Winter Season)

The best time to visit Nainital and Ranikhet is during the summer months of March to June when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. Winter (October to February) is also a great time to visit if you want to experience snowfall and enjoy the chilly weather.

How to Reach

By Air: To reach Nainital and Ranikhet, the nearest major airport is the Pantnagar Airport, located approximately 70 kms away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a shared cab to reach your destination.

By Road: Nainital and Ranikhet are well-connected by road. Regular bus services operate from Delhi, Dehradun, and other major cities in Uttarakhand. Private taxis and self-drive cars can also be hired to reach these destinations.

By Train: The nearest railway station to Nainital is Kathgodam, which is approximately 34 kms away. Kathgodam is well-connected to major cities in India. From Kathgodam, you can hire a taxi or take a shared cab to reach Nainital. Ranikhet can be reached via the same route.

Things to Do

1. Nainital:

- Enjoy a boat ride on the scenic Naini Lake.

- Visit the famous Naina Devi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi.

- Explore the Nainital Zoo, home to various Himalayan species.

- Take a cable car ride to Snow View Point for stunning views of the snow-clad mountains.

- Shop for unique handicrafts and souvenirs at Mall Road.

2. Ranikhet:

- Visit the famous Jhula Devi Temple, known for its bells and religious significance.

- Explore the Chaubatia Gardens, known for their orchards and panoramic views.

- Take a walk along the Ranikhet Golf Course, one of the highest golf courses in Asia.

Places to Stay

Nainital and Ranikhet offer a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From luxury resorts and hotels to budget guesthouses and homestays, you will find plenty of choices.

Safety Tips

While visiting Nainital and Ranikhet, it is important to keep the following safety tips in mind:

- Carry sufficient warm clothing, especially during the winter months, as the temperatures can drop significantly.

- Stay hydrated and carry a water bottle with you, especially if you plan to engage in outdoor activities.

- Follow the instructions and guidelines provided by local authorities and trekking guides during hikes and treks.

- Be cautious while boating and follow all safety instructions provided by boat operators.

- Avoid venturing into isolated areas or hiking alone, particularly during late hours.

- Take necessary precautions to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other insects, especially during the monsoon season.

- Respect the local customs, traditions, and wildlife. Do not litter and help maintain the cleanliness of the surroundings.

Local Cuisine:

While exploring Nainital and Ranikhet, don’t miss the opportunity to savour the local cuisine. The hill stations offer a variety of dishes that will tantalize your taste buds. Some popular local delicacies include:

- Bal Mithai: A sweet made with roasted khoya, coated with sugar balls, and served in a leaf.

- Aloo ke Gutke: Spicy and tangy stir-fried potatoes with local flavors and herbs.

- Bhatt ki Churkani: A traditional Kumaoni curry made with black soybeans and a blend of spices.

- Baadi: A famous local bread made with fermented black lentil batter, served with chutney or curry.

- Ras: A refreshing beverage made from local fruits like rhododendron, plum, or apricot.

Nearby Attractions

While exploring Nainital and Ranikhet, you can also visit the following nearby attractions:

- Bhimtal: Known for its picturesque lake and the Bhimeshwara Mahadev Temple.

- Almora: A scenic hill town famous for its rich cultural heritage and stunning views of the Himalayas.

- Kausani: Renowned for its panoramic views of the Nanda Devi and Trishul peaks.

- Jim Corbett National Park: India’s oldest national park, famous for its diverse wildlife and the majestic Bengal tiger.

3. Jim Corbett — Where Wildlife Roams Free

Jim Corbett National Park, established in 1936, is the oldest national park in India. Named after the renowned conservationist and author Jim Corbett, it is located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand. The park spans across the foothills of the Himalayas and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Why Visit: Experience thrilling encounters with majestic tigers and other exotic wildlife amidst the picturesque landscapes of Jim Corbett National Park.

Ideal for: Adventure enthusiasts, nature lovers, and wildlife photographers seeking an immersive wildlife experience.

Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Jim Corbett National Park is from November to June when the weather is pleasant and wildlife sightings are more frequent.

Ideal Duration: A 2–3 day visit to Jim Corbett is recommended to explore the park’s various zones and engage in wildlife safaris.

How to Reach: Jim Corbett can be reached by flights to Pantnagar Airport, followed by a road journey of approximately 2–3 hours. Regular bus services and private taxis are also available from nearby cities like Delhi and Dehradun.

Places to Stay: Luxury Hotels/Resorts: The Gateway Resort, Jim’s Jungle Retreat, The Solluna Resort Budget Hotels: Corbett Machaan Resort, Corbett Aroma Park, The Wild Heritage

Things to Do:

- Enjoy thrilling wildlife safaris and jeep excursions to spot tigers, elephants, deer, and various bird species.

- Take a serene nature walk amidst the lush forests and pristine rivers of the park.

- Visit the Corbett Museum to learn about the life and legacy of Jim Corbett.

Safety Tips:

- Follow the instructions given by the park authorities during wildlife safaris.

- Maintain a safe distance from wild animals and avoid feeding them.

- Carry necessary medications, insect repellents, and first aid kits.

Nearby Attractions:

- Garjiya Devi Temple: A revered temple located on the banks of the Kosi River, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys.

- Corbett Waterfall: A scenic waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, perfect for picnics and relaxation.

- Sitabani Temple: A historical temple dedicated to Goddess Sita, known for its tranquil ambiance and spiritual significance.

4. Almora — Famous Offbeat Places

Almora, nestled amidst the Kumaon Hills in Uttarakhand, is a captivating hill station renowned for its rich history and cultural heritage. Established in the 16th century by the Chand dynasty, Almora boasts ancient temples, intricate handicrafts, and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Why Visit Almora:

Uncover the Charm of Serene Tranquility

Ideal for:

Nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and spiritual seekers

Best Time to Visit Almora:

( March to June and September to November )

Ideal Duration:

Spend at least 3–4 days exploring the wonders of Almora

How to Reach Almora:

- By Flights: The nearest airport is Pantnagar Airport, approximately 115 km away.

- By Roads: Well-connected by road, Almora can be accessed through regular bus services or by hiring a private taxi.

- By Train: Kathgodam Railway Station is the nearest railhead, about 90 km from Almora.

Things to Do in Almora:

- Explore Bright End Corner: Witness breathtaking sunrise and sunset views from this viewpoint while sipping on a hot cup of tea.

- Visit Kasar Devi Temple: Seek blessings at this ancient temple known for its spiritual aura and panoramic vistas.

- Trek to Zero Point: Embark on an adventurous trek to Zero Point for spectacular views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks.

Places to Stay in Almora:

a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

Safety Tips for Almora:

- Carry warm clothing, as the weather can get chilly, especially during winters.

- Stay hydrated and carry water bottles during your sightseeing excursions.

- Respect the local customs and traditions and maintain decorum in religious places.

Nearby Attractions:

- Jageshwar: Visit the ancient Jageshwar Temple complex, famous for its cluster of 124 shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva.

- Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: Immerse yourself in nature’s splendor and spot various wildlife species in this picturesque sanctuary.

- Ranikhet: Explore the serene hill station of Ranikhet, known for its lush green meadows, ancient temples, and stunning views of the Himalayas.

5. Auli — Clad Slopes of Auli.

Auli, located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, is a picturesque hill station known for its breathtaking views and thrilling ski slopes. It is nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas and offers a serene escape from the bustling city life.

Why visit: Experience the adrenaline rush of skiing down the pristine white slopes, surrounded by the magnificent Himalayan peaks.

Ideal for: Adventure enthusiasts seeking a thrilling skiing experience in the lap of nature.

Best time to visit: November to March, during the winter season when the snowfall blankets the region.

Ideal duration: 4–5 days, allowing ample time to indulge in skiing and explore the scenic beauty of the surroundings.

How to reach:

- By air: The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which is well-connected to major cities in India. From there, one can hire a taxi or take a bus to Auli.

- By road: Auli is well-connected by road, and regular buses and taxis are available from major cities in Uttarakhand.

- By train: The nearest railway station is Rishikesh, which is approximately 250 km away from Auli. From Rishikesh, one can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Auli.

Things to do:

- Skiing: Enjoy the thrill of skiing on the well-groomed slopes of Auli, which offer a perfect terrain for both beginners and experienced skiers.

- Cable car ride: Take a mesmerizing cable car ride from Joshimath to Auli, witnessing panoramic views of the Himalayas along the way.

- Trekking: Embark on scenic treks in the vicinity, such as the Kuari Pass Trek or the Valley of Flowers Trek.

- Visit Gurso Bugyal: Explore the scenic meadow of Gurso Bugyal, known for its lush greenery and stunning views of Nanda Devi.

Places to stay:

- Luxury hotels/resorts: The Clifftop Club, Auli Resort, and The Royal Village are some of the luxury options offering comfortable accommodation and stunning views.

- Budget hotels: GMVN Auli, The Tattva Resort, and The Devi Darshan Lodge provide affordable yet cozy stay options.

- Dharmshalas: GMVN Joshi math and Auli, as well as various local dharmshalas, offer budget-friendly accommodation.

Safety tips:

- Carry proper skiing gear, including helmets, goggles, and appropriate clothing, to ensure safety on the slopes.

- Stay hydrated and carry snacks to maintain energy levels during skiing sessions.

- Follow the instructions of trained instructors and avoid venturing into unmarked areas.

Nearby attractions:

- Joshimath: Visit this town, situated close to Auli, known for its religious significance and the ancient temple of Narasimha.

- Badrinath: Pay a visit to the holy town of Badrinath, home to the famous Badrinath Temple and surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty.

- Valley of Flowers National Park: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its vibrant alpine flowers and diverse flora and fauna.

Additional points:

- Auli is one of the highest skiing destinations in India, offering a challenging terrain for professionals and beginners alike.

- The Skiing Festival held in Auli attracts skiing enthusiasts from across the globe.

- Auli also offers breathtaking views of the Nanda Devi, Kamet, and Mana Parvat peaks.

- Adventure activities like snowboarding and snow trekking are also popular in Auli.

- The Auli Artificial Lake, developed to provide artificial snow for skiing, is a popular tourist spot.

Chakrata (Hilltown) — Embrace Serenity in Chakrata

Chakrata is a serene hilltown located in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, India. It was initially established as a British military outpost in 1866 and later served as a summer retreat due to its pleasant climate and picturesque surroundings.

Why Visit Chakrata: Experience tranquility amidst nature’s beauty and explore offbeat trails in Chakrata.

Ideal for: Nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those seeking a peaceful getaway.

Best Time to Visit: (March to June and from September to November)

Ideal Duration: at least 2 to 3 days

Places to Stay in Chakrata: Chakrata offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets like Luxury Hotels/Resorts, Budget Hotels & Lodges are available.

How to Reach Chakrata:

Chakrata can be reached by road from major nearby cities like Dehradun and Delhi. The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, approximately 115 kilometers away. The town is well-connected by buses, and rental cars/taxis are available for convenient transportation. The nearest railway station is Dehradun Railway Station.

Things to Do in Chakrata:

- Enjoy panoramic views from Tiger Falls, a majestic waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.

- Trek to Deoban, a picturesque location known for its dense forests and stunning views of the Himalayas.

- Visit the ancient Mahasu Devta Temple and soak in the spiritual ambiance.

- Explore the hidden gem of Chilmiri Neck, offering breathtaking vistas of the surrounding valleys.

Safety Tips for Chakrata:

- Carry sufficient warm clothing, especially during winters, as the temperatures can drop significantly.
